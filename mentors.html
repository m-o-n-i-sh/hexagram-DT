Mentoring Team

Four practitioners from Mahindra University's Design Thinking faculty who pushed us to go deeper at every stage — from the first empathy interview to the final working prototype.

R
Raj Narayanan
Assistant Professor, Design Thinking

Mr. Raj Narayanan holds an M.Des in Visual Design from IIT Hyderabad and a B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering (Industrial stream) from the College of Engineering, Thiruvananthapuram. Before joining Mahindra University, he worked as a UX Designer at My Medicare Pvt. Ltd. His interests span collaborative design, design methods, form explorations, semiotics, and physical interactions. Through this project he pushed us to treat every user interview as a design artefact in itself, not just a data-gathering exercise.

S
Sayoni Laha
Assistant Professor, School of Design Innovation

Ms. Sayoni Laha holds an M.F.A. from the University of Hyderabad and a B.F.A. from the University of Burdwan. She is Head of Marketing, People Supply Chain and Corporate Services at Mahindra University's School of Design Innovation, and brings eight years of teaching experience alongside an active practice as an exhibiting artist. Her research interests include design for the elderly, women and society, and heritage-rooted design. During this project she kept asking us to make our ideas more specific to the person in front of us, not to a generalised user.

A
Abhishek
Design Mentor, Design Thinking

A core member of Mahindra University's Design Thinking faculty, Abhishek's sessions centred on systems thinking and the relationship between how you frame a problem and the quality of the solutions it produces. He was particularly sharp on root cause analysis, consistently asking us whether the cause we had identified was actually a cause or just a symptom wearing a cause's disguise. That rigour shaped a lot of our RCA work.

S
Shijith VP
Design Mentor, Design Thinking

Dr. Shijith VP holds a PhD from the Department of Design at IIT Hyderabad, with research interests in cinema, photography, gender, and health. At Mahindra University he mentors teams on prototyping discipline and the gap between what teams think they are building and what users actually experience. His feedback during our prototype review sessions was the most direct we received, and the most useful.
