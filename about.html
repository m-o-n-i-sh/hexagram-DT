About the Course

Design Thinking is more than a methodology — it is a discipline for engineering empathy into the things we build.

The studio

The Design Thinking studio at Mahindra University is a hands-on, project-based course where student teams are immersed in the full cycle of human-centered design: discovering real problems through field research, defining sharp problem statements, ideating broadly, prototyping rapidly, and testing relentlessly with real users.

Over the course of a semester, each team picks up a thread of human experience — a gap, a frustration, an unmet need — and follows it across interviews, conversations, observations, and analysis until a solution begins to take shape. The work is messy by design. Insight does not arrive on a schedule.

What we practice

  • Empathy as a method. Listening, observing, and resisting the urge to jump to solutions.
  • Problem framing. Translating raw observations into How Might We questions that open up the design space.
  • Divergent thinking. Generating a large quantity of ideas before converging on the most promising ones.
  • Prototyping. Making the abstract tangible — fast, rough, and good enough to learn from.
  • Iteration. Treating every prototype as a question and every test as an answer.

The process

Teams move through five overlapping phases: Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, and Test. The arc is not linear. Discoveries late in the process often send teams back to the field, and a sharp insight in week eight can reframe everything that came before. The course rewards intellectual honesty over polish.

Why it matters for engineers

Engineering education tends to optimize for solutions. Design thinking optimizes for problems — for noticing them, defining them well, and choosing the right ones to solve. For computer science engineers in particular, the course is a counterweight: a reminder that technology is at its best when it answers a real human question.
